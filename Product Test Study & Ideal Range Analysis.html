Product Test Study & Ideal Range Analysis

Context: Before launching new products, we needed to validate that key sensory attributes (like sweetness or texture) matched consumer expectations. Without structured testing, formulation decisions were often subjective and delayed.

Solution: Led multiple product testing studies using the Ideal Range Analysis framework:

  • Applied Quadratic Regression to map consumer liking against attribute intensity
  • Identified the Ideal Range between Ideal Low and Ideal High
  • Used global norm benchmarks to guide interpretation and ensure competitive relevance
  • Collected consumer feedback on both intensity and liking in a structured, scalable format

Impact:

  • Refined product formulas to match optimal consumer preferences
  • Accelerated decision-making in the New Product Development (NPD) pipeline
  • Identified attribute gaps (e.g., “too sweet” or “not chewy enough”) that became clear business insight for R&D and marketing
  • Enabled confident, insight-led launches of new products that met consumer expectations
  • Supported go-to-market readiness by aligning sensory profiles with commercial targets
  • Contributed to business growth through successful product launches grounded in consumer data
