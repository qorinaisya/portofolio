Packaging Evaluation Using Eye Tracking

Context: A brand planned to update its packaging to reduce production costs. The key question: Would consumers notice the change, and how would it affect perception?

Solution: We designed a two-phase evaluation leveraging portable eye tracking:

  • On-Shelf Test: Tracked consumer eye movements to assess visibility and attention among competing products.
  • In-Hand Evaluation: Observed reactions once consumers physically inspected the pack.

Impact:

  • Eye tracking showed the new pack went largely unnoticed on shelf
  • Upon inspection, consumers perceived the change negatively, associating it with smaller size or reduced quality
  • Insights helped the brand reconsider design changes, protecting brand perception while balancing cost efficiency
